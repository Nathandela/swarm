How the phone reaches your computers

A Self-hosted E2EE relay RECOMMENDED

Daemons and phone dial out TLS/443 to a small relay you run; every payload end-to-end encrypted, relay stores ciphertext only. Push rides through as ciphertext, decrypted on-device. Works anywhere, app killed or not. Cost: one VPS + owning the crypto (Noise XX; Happy Coder is the working precedent).

D Tailscale transport + blind push broker

The audit committee's honest challenger: phone talks straight to daemons over your tailnet (WireGuard E2EE, identity and revocation handled by Tailscale); a tiny stateless broker only signs and forwards opaque wake-up pushes. Less custom crypto; requires the Tailscale app and tolerates VPN reconnect lag when opening.

B/C Pure Tailscale / WebRTC

Pure tailnet fails the core requirement — nothing can wake the phone. WebRTC adds signaling + TURN infra for latency the terminal-text workload can't feel. Both rejected for v1.
